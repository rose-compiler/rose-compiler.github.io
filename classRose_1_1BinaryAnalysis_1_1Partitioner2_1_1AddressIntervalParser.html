Rose::BinaryAnalysis::Partitioner2::AddressIntervalParser Class Reference

Description

Parse an address interval.

An interval is specified in one of the following forms, where N and M are non-negative integers:

  • "N" is a singleton interval.
  • "N,M" is the min/max form specifying the two inclusive end points. M must not be less than N.
  • "N-M" is the begin/end form where M is greater than N and not included in the interval.
  • "N+M" is the base/size form where N is the minimum value and M is the number of items.

The integers can be specified in decimal, octal, hexadecimal, or binary using the usual C/C++ syntax.
